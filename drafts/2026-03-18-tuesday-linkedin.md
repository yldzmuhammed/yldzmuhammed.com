# Series: IoT Fundamentals | Week 1

---

Most people think IoT is just connecting a sensor to the internet.

It's not. It's a communication problem. And communication has physics behind it.

Think about shouting to someone across a field. The further they are, the louder you have to shout, and the faster your throat gets tired.

Your device does the exact same thing. The further it transmits, the more power it burns. That's not a software bug you can optimize away. That's physics.

Now imagine a crowded room where everyone is talking at once. Conversations break down. People repeat themselves. Data is lost.

That's what happens when too many devices share the same radio frequency without a structured protocol to manage who talks when.

This is what IoT is really about: devices that need to communicate reliably, across distance, on a limited power budget, in a world full of interference.

Every protocol you've heard of, LoRaWAN, BLE, LTE, Wi-Fi, Zigbee, exists because each one solves this problem differently. Different range. Different power. Different cost. Different tradeoffs.

Over the next few months I'm going to break down each one. How it works, what the hardware implications are, what it actually costs to ship at scale, and when to walk away from it.

If you're building connected hardware, or thinking about it, follow along.
